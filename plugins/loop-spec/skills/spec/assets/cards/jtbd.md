# JTBD — spec card

The main job the product is hired for, the success criteria users judge it by, and the user scenarios that show the job in action. It stays at the level of the job — no problem statement, no metrics, no solution or feature spec. The scenarios are the core: each concrete enough to build toward and to check a build against. The main job and criteria stay durable across feature changes; the scenarios stay sharp and build-pointing. The doc describes the target state, not the current one.

**Template:** [../templates/jtbd.md](../templates/jtbd.md) · **Saves as:** `jtbd.md`

## Ground in

What the product is *for* — the README, the app, and the code where it sharpens the picture, plus what the user tells you about the product and its users. When there's little to ground in, say so and work from what the user provides; the job and scenarios still ground in something real.

## Analysis method

From the product's purpose, work out what the product is really hired for: the main job, the success criteria users judge it by, and the 5–8 situations where the job shows up. This is the reasoning move — what the job *is* — done before it's committed to the draft. Name the job the product serves, not the solution that serves it.

## Drafting notes

Each scenario must be concrete enough to build toward and to check a build against — resolve the vague ones before presenting. The honest account states where the job and scenarios are solid, where they rest on assumptions about intent, and what's known versus unknown.

## Facilitation angles

- Whether the job is framed at the right altitude and scope at all.
- Whether it fits the user's real intent better or worse than the draft assumes.
- Whether the conversation points to a stronger framing of the job — or a more telling set of scenarios — than the draft captured.

## Standards

- **Job-level** — names the job users hire the product for, not the features that serve it.
- **Durable core** — the main job and success criteria hold across feature changes.
- **Build-pointing scenarios** — each is concrete enough to build toward and to check a build against.
- **Grounded** — the job and scenarios trace to the product's real purpose, not invented personas.

## Verify focus

Stress-test the main job, success criteria, and scenarios — where they don't hold against the product's real purpose.
