# Requirements — spec card

Turn one idea into genuinely different, grounded ways to build it, then settle on one. The input is one idea: named in the prompt, or a path to an idea or ideation doc. This doc is generative — the analysis manufactures options rather than reading structure out of code.

**Template:** [../templates/requirements.md](../templates/requirements.md) · **Saves as:** `requirements.md`

## Ground in

The idea itself. Recap it back — problem, proposal, constraints, known downsides — in plain language, and confirm the understanding before analyzing.

## Analysis method

Examine the idea from several angles in session — first principles (what problem is really being solved), compared-to-what (the cheaper or existing alternative), the assumptions and constraints it takes as fixed, a premortem (how this fails), and the smallest honest form of it. Read across the angles for where they align, where they diverge, and which assumptions or constraints they question.

Then write **three interpretations that are substantively different** — each resting on a different assumption questioned or constraint changed, not three sizes of one idea. Each stands alone, jargon-free, with:

- **Goal** — what this version is really trying to achieve.
- **Proposal** — what you'd actually build, in plain terms.
- **Scope** — what's in, and what's deliberately left out.
- **What it prioritizes** — what this version trades for what.

Close with a plain explanation of what is fundamentally different between the three. Check firsthand any fact the interpretations hinge on before they go out — assertions from memory turn out half-true.

## Drafting notes

Present the options before any call is made: contextualize the whole picture plainly, then compare the three side by side in a feature-comparison table with honest tradeoffs. The honest account gives your read of where each is strong and what it rests on — not yet a recommendation; the call comes in facilitation. The saved doc captures the chosen direction and the decisions behind it, per the template.

## Facilitation angles

- Whether these are even the right framings.
- Whether one fits the user's intent better than it first looked.
- Whether two should merge, or the analysis points to a stronger direction none of them captured.

The final direction can be one of the three, a reshaped version, or something new the conversation reached.

## Standards

- **Substantively different options** — each interpretation rests on a different questioned assumption or changed constraint, not a size gradient.
- **Grounded** — options and the facts they hinge on are verified, not asserted from memory.
- **Decision-carrying** — the settled doc records the chosen direction, the trade-offs made, and what's deliberately out.
- **At requirements altitude** — what and why, not implementation.

## Verify focus

Stress-test the chosen direction and the trade-offs it rests on. Keep feedback at the level of a requirements doc — ignore or adapt anything implementation-focused.
